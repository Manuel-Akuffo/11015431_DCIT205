Undergraduate programmes

The Department offers two undergraduate degree programmes, Bachelor of Science in Computer Science Single-Major (3:2:1:1) and Major-Minor (3:2:2:1) as well as Bachelor of Science in Information Technology. To qualify for admission into Computer Science programmes at Level 200, candidates must have taken the following courses at Level 100: MATH 121: Algebra and Trigonometry MATH 122: Calculus I CSCD 101: Introduction to Computer Science I CSCD 102: Introduction to Computer Science II Every student in the Department of Computer Science must own his or her laptop with minimum specification of: i3 64-bit Processor 8GB RAM 500GB HD or 256 SSD

PhD programmes

Artificial Intelligence (AI): Advanced topics in machine learning, natural language processing, computer vision, and robotics. Machine Learning: Development of algorithms and models for computers to learn patterns from data. Data Science: Techniques for collecting, processing, analyzing, and interpreting large datasets. Computer Networks and Distributed Systems: Design, analysis, and optimization of computer networks and distributed computing systems. Software Engineering: Advanced research in software design, development methodologies, testing, and maintenance. Computer Vision: Algorithms and systems for computers to interpret visual information from the world. Human-Computer Interaction (HCI): Design and evaluation of user interfaces and interactive systems. Cybersecurity: Development of secure systems, cryptography, network security, and cyber threat protection. Computer Graphics: Creation and manipulation of visual images and animations, including virtual reality. Theoretical Computer Science: Mathematical and theoretical aspects of computing, including algorithms and complexity theory. Database Systems: Advanced research in database design, data management, and scalable systems. Quantum Computing: Principles and applications of quantum mechanics for quantum computers and algorithms. Natural Language Processing (NLP): Development of algorithms and models for understanding and processing human language. MPhil and Msc programmes

Explore our diverse MPhil and Msc programme areas

  • Master of Philosophy (MPhil) Programs:

    In-depth research-oriented master's programs. Focus on developing research skills and contributing to the academic field. Typically involves coursework, comprehensive exams, and a research thesis. May provide a pathway for students interested in pursuing a Ph.D. Opportunities to work closely with faculty on research projects. Duration varies but usually takes 1 to 2 years to complete.

  • Master of Science (MSc) Programs:

    Comprehensive master's programs with a balance of coursework and research. Aimed at providing advanced knowledge in specific areas of Computer Science. Common specializations include artificial intelligence, data science, cybersecurity, and software engineering. Typically includes a capstone project or thesis component. Designed to prepare students for professional roles in industry or research. Duration varies but generally takes 1 to 2 years to complete. May offer opportunities for internships or industry collaborations.
